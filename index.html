Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Aging is a big factor in cognitive function. Much like the rest of the body, the brain actually shrinks as you age.The most apparent age-related cognitive change is memory.

You can keep your brain running smoothly by keeping it in shape, the same way you do your body—play memory games, eat a healthy diet, and wear hearing aids if you suffer from hearing loss!

The greater one’s income, the lower one’s likelihood of disease and premature death. Studies show that Americans at all income levels are less healthy than those with incomes higher than their own.Not onlyis income (the earnings and other money acquired each year) associated with better health, but wealth (net worth and assets) affects health as well.

Overweight and obesity may increase the risk of many health problems, including diabetes, heart disease, and certain cancers. If you are pregnant, excess weight may lead to short- and long-term health problems for you and your child.
